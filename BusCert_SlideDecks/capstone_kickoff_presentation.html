What Actually Counts?

Hours:15+ hours/week
Duration:10+ weeks (Spring) / 8+ weeks (Summer)
Compensation:Paid preferred, unpaid acceptable if it meets criteria
Supervisor:Must have an employer point person (not immediate family)
Work Product:Identifiable project you'll complete
Skills:Specific skills you'll develop
Function:Finance, Marketing, Strategy, Consulting, Tech, Operations, HR, Sales, etc.

Ideally links to your career goals.

Think Creatively

Yes, apply to your dream companies AND explore creative options:

  • A family member's business that needs a social media campaign or marketing strategy
  • A small business owner who needs help streamlining operations or processes
  • A nonprofit in your community that needs fundraising support or program development
  • A local restaurant that wants to improve their online presence
  • Someone self-employed who needs basic business infrastructure (invoicing, client tracking, etc.)

Who do you know? Family, friends, community members, former employers?
Having multiple options gives you a better chance of securing something in time.

Common Questions

  • ❓ "I already have a job—does my Starbucks position count?"
    Maybe. It depends on your role and what projects you can do. Let's discuss it.
  • ❓ "I'm doing research in a lab—does that count?"
    Usually No! But talk to us before deciding.
  • ❓ "Can I work for a family business?"
    Yes! But you need a supervisor who is NOT an immediate family member.
  • ❓ "Can I do a virtual/remote internship?"
    Absolutely.
  • ❓ "Can I combine two shorter internships?"
    Usually No! But talk to us before deciding.

Stay Organized: Job Tracker

We're providing you with a Job Tracker spreadsheet to help you manage your search:

What it tracks:

  • Job Applications - Every position you apply to, status updates, interview dates
  • Contacts - Professional connections, networking contacts, follow-up reminders
  • Follow-ups - Scheduled outreach to recruiters and hiring managers
  • Resources - Useful links, job boards, career prep materials

Job Search Strategy

  • Apply broadly and often — Don't just send 5 applications and give up. Aim for 10+ per week.
  • Tailor your applications — Customize your resume and cover letter for each opportunity
  • Start with your network — Ask: "Is there anyone else I can speak to?" Create a chain
  • Be open! — Your dream role might come from an unexpected place
  • Have a Plan A AND backup plans — Especially if you're close to graduation
  • Remember the dead period — Between Thanksgiving and New Year's, hiring slows down AND you're busy with Finals. Apply NOW!
